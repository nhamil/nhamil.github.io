About Me

Hi! My name is Nicholas Hamilton. I am a computer science PhD candidate at Michigan Technological University studying machine learning. Currently, I am exploring augmentation methods for object detection. I began working towards my computer science batchelor's degree in September 2016 and graduated in December 2019 with a department GPA of 4.00 and a cumulative GPA of 3.78.

I have had a variety of programming interests over the years, mainly focusing on machine learning, computer graphics, procedual generation, and physics simulations. Some of my other hobbies include worldbuilding, sketching, chess, and shorthand.
