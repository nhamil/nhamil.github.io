About Me

Hi! My name is Nicholas Hamilton. I am a computer science PhD candidate at Michigan Technological University studying machine learning. Currently, I am using computer vision to improve flood modeling. I have also explored synthetic augmentation methods for object detection. I began working towards my computer science batchelor's degree in September 2016 and graduated in December 2019 with a department GPA of 4.00 and a cumulative GPA of 3.78. I got my master's degree in December 2022 with a GPA of 4.00.

I have had a variety of programming interests over the years, mainly focusing on machine learning, game development, computer graphics, procedural generation, and physics simulations. Some of my other hobbies include worldbuilding, chess, sketching, and shorthand.
